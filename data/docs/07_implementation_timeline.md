# EU AI Act — Implementation Timeline

The Regulation entered into force on 1 August 2024, twenty days after publication in the Official Journal on 12 July 2024. Its provisions apply on a staggered schedule defined in Article 113.

## 2 February 2025 — six months after entry into force

The following apply:

- Chapter I — general provisions (subject matter, scope, definitions).
- Chapter II — prohibited AI practices (Article 5).
- Article 4 — AI literacy: providers and deployers must ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy among staff and any other persons dealing with the operation and use of AI systems on their behalf, taking into account technical knowledge, experience, education, training and the context in which the systems are to be used.

## 2 August 2025 — twelve months after entry into force

The following apply:

- Chapter III Section 4 — notifying authorities and notified bodies.
- Chapter V — general-purpose AI models (Articles 51–56).
- Chapter VII — governance (the AI Office, the AI Board, the advisory forum, the scientific panel).
- Chapter XII — penalties (Article 99 administrative fines, Article 101 fines on GPAI providers; Article 100 EDPS fines on EU institutions).
- Article 78 — confidentiality.

Member States must designate national competent authorities, communicate them to the Commission, and publish their contact details by this date.

## 2 August 2026 — twenty-four months after entry into force

The bulk of the Regulation applies, including:

- All obligations on high-risk AI systems listed in Annex III (the eight stand-alone use-case categories).
- Transparency obligations for limited-risk systems (Article 50).
- Most obligations on providers, deployers, importers, distributors, authorised representatives.
- The obligation to register high-risk systems in the EU database.
- Member States must by this date establish at least one operational AI regulatory sandbox at national level (Article 57).

## 2 August 2027 — thirty-six months after entry into force

- Article 6(1) — high-risk classification rules and obligations apply to AI systems that are safety components of products covered by Union harmonisation legislation listed in Annex I (e.g. medical devices, machinery, toys, in-vitro diagnostics). The longer transition reflects existing certification cycles in those product regimes.
- GPAI models placed on the market before 2 August 2025 must come into compliance with Chapter V by this date.

## 31 December 2030

AI systems that are components of large-scale IT systems established by EU acts listed in Annex X (e.g. SIS, VIS, Eurodac, ETIAS, ECRIS-TCN) and that have been placed on the market before 2 August 2027 must come into compliance by this date.

## Other key milestones

- 2 August 2028 — first Commission evaluation report on the functioning of the Regulation. Subsequent reviews every four years.
- Codes of Practice for GPAI must be ready by 2 May 2025 (nine months after entry into force) so that they can underpin compliance from 2 August 2025.
