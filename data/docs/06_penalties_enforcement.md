# EU AI Act — Penalties and Enforcement

Chapter XII of the Regulation (Articles 99–101) establishes a tiered penalty regime, supplemented by Member-State rules on additional administrative and criminal sanctions where consistent with EU law.

## Administrative fines (Article 99)

Three penalty bands apply to providers, deployers, importers, distributors and notified bodies, depending on the obligation breached. For each band the higher of the two amounts is the cap; SMEs and start-ups face the lower of the two amounts.

| Breach | Maximum fine |
|---|---|
| Non-compliance with the prohibition of AI practices in Article 5 | €35 million or 7% of total worldwide annual turnover for the preceding financial year |
| Non-compliance with most other obligations of providers, authorised representatives, importers, distributors, deployers, notified bodies (excluding Article 5) | €15 million or 3% of worldwide annual turnover |
| Supply of incorrect, incomplete or misleading information to notified bodies or national competent authorities | €7.5 million or 1% of worldwide annual turnover |

When deciding on the amount, authorities must take into account the nature, gravity and duration of the infringement, whether other authorities have already imposed fines for the same infringement, the size and market share of the operator, intentional or negligent character, the degree of cooperation, and any benefit gained.

## Fines on Union institutions, bodies, offices and agencies (Article 100)

The European Data Protection Supervisor may impose fines on EU institutions, bodies, offices and agencies: up to €1.5 million for prohibited-practice breaches; up to €750 000 for other breaches.

## Fines on providers of GPAI models (Article 101)

The European Commission, on a proposal from the AI Office, may impose fines on GPAI providers of up to €15 million or 3% of worldwide annual turnover, for intentional or negligent infringements of Chapter V, failure to comply with information requests, failure to make a model available for evaluation, or failure to comply with mitigation measures. The Commission must give the provider an opportunity to be heard and may issue non-binding findings before deciding.

## Enforcement architecture

National market-surveillance authorities under Regulation (EU) 2019/1020 supervise AI systems within their territory. Each Member State must designate at least one notifying authority and one market-surveillance authority. The European AI Office, within the European Commission, is exclusively competent for supervising and enforcing GPAI obligations. The European Artificial Intelligence Board, composed of one representative per Member State, coordinates national authorities, issues opinions and recommendations, and supports consistent application across the Union. A scientific panel of independent experts assists the AI Office, in particular for systemic-risk evaluation.

## Rights of affected persons

Article 85 grants any natural or legal person the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant market-surveillance authority concerning non-compliance with the Regulation. Article 86 grants persons subject to a decision taken on the basis of a high-risk AI system listed in Annex III (with limited exceptions) the right to obtain from the deployer a clear and meaningful explanation of the role of the AI system in the decision-making procedure and the main elements of the decision taken. The Whistleblower Directive (EU) 2019/1937 applies to reports of breaches.
